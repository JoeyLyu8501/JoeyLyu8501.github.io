Zizhou Lyu

2002, Born in Jinan, China
| Architecture Student at Syracuse University | Developer of Minecraft China Edition(NetEase) | Voxel Artist |

Zizhou Lyu is presently engaged in a 5-year Bachelor of Architecture program at Syracuse University, while concurrently minoring in Information Studies. In addition to his scholastic pursuits, Lyu has distinguished himself as a voxel artist and serves as a Developer for the Chinese edition of Minecraft managed by NetEase. Furthermore, he holds the position of Co-Founder of Voxel Utopia, a platform dedicated to the exchange and collaboration among early-stage voxel artists and creative teams within the Minecraft ecosystem. Originally conceived as "Minecraft Big Bath," Voxel Utopia aims to consolidate disparate artistic entities and facilitate operational collaborations among independent teams. To that end, the platform has launched an integrated server environment equipped with a comprehensive set of design resources and plugins, thus enabling concurrent creative activities among hundreds of artists. In recognition of his individual contributions to voxel art, Zizhou Lyu received a third-place award in the Future City Design Competition, a collaborative initiative organized in 2018 by the Global Institute for Sustainable Development at Tsinghua University and the Korean Urban Regeneration Center. Currently, Zizhou Lyu is directing a project known as Voxel Campus, which seeks to create voxel-based renditions of iconic architectural landmarks from various academic institutions.
